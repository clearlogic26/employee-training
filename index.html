1. Welcome Start here before your first shift +

PWI is fast-moving frozen dessert shops on the Wildwood Boardwalk. Your job is to keep service quick, accurate, and friendly while helping guests choose from water ice, soft serve, chillers, shakes, floats, and drinks.

What matters most

Be friendly, stay clean, move with purpose, and double-check every order before handing it out.

Boardwalk mindset

Summer nights get busy fast. Calm communication and smooth teamwork keep the line moving.

Before day one

Study the products, prices, and quiz so you can start your first shift with the basics locked in.

2. Our Products Know what every item is before you serve it +

Guests often ask for descriptions, flavor help, and combo suggestions. Learn these definitions so you can explain them fast and confidently.

PWI (Polish Water Ice)

Frozen fruit-flavored ice eaten with a spoon. Guests can mix any 2 flavors in one cup. Cotton Candy is the top seller.

Cherry Blue Raspberry Cotton Candy Watermelon Mango Piña Colada Chocolate Lemon Green Apple

Polish Freeze

Combo cup with water ice on one side and ice cream on the other, similar to a gelati. Uses the same 9 PWI flavors plus ice cream.

Chiller

Blended ice cream dessert, similar to a DQ Blizzard. Bases are vanilla, chocolate, or twist.

Oreo M&Ms Reeses PB Cups Snickers Reeses Pieces Butterfinger

Milkshake

Classic blended shake in small or large.

Cookies & Cream Vanilla Chocolate Black & White Strawberry Banana Root Beer Cherry Blue Raspberry Watermelon Cotton Candy Mango Piña Colada

Ice Cream

Soft serve in chocolate, vanilla, or twist. Served in a cup, regular cone, or waffle cone. Sprinkles are an add-on.

Slushie

Any PWI flavor blended with a little water. It is the drinkable version of water ice.

Ice Cap

Water ice float made with any PWI flavor plus a soda choice.

Pepsi Diet Pepsi Starry Mountain Dew Ginger Ale Orange Crush Root Beer Gatorade

Float

Classic float made with ice cream and soda.

Beverages

We also sell bottled water, soda, and Gatorade for guests who want a simple drink.

4. Opening Checklist Use this before the first customers arrive +
  1. Turn on the lights.
  2. Clock in.
  3. Turn on the machines.
  4. Stir Piña Colada, Mango, Chocolate.
  5. Wring out the rags under the machines.
5. Closing Checklist Reset the shop for the next day +
  • Clean all machines thoroughly.
  • Wipe down all surfaces.
  • Restock supplies for the next day.
  • Empty trash cans.
  • Turn off all machines and lights.
  • Clock out.
  • Lock the store.
6. Customer Service Tips Fast, friendly, accurate service +
  • Always greet customers with a smile.
  • Be proactive. Don't wait for customers to figure out the menu - ask what they're in the mood for and suggest items. "Have you tried our Polish Freeze? It's water ice and ice cream together!" goes a long way.
  • If you do not know an answer, ask the manager. Do not guess.
  • Repeat the order back before making it.
  • Speed matters on busy nights because the boardwalk line moves fast.
  • Keep your station clean between orders.
  • Suggest popular combos like: "Want to try our Polish Freeze? It's our water ice and ice cream together!"
  • Never argue with a customer. Stay calm and be polite. If a situation escalates, apologize and offer to make it right. There is no manager on site.
7. Phone Policy Customer awareness comes first +

The #1 Rule: If you can see their eyes, you greet them. Every person who looks toward or approaches the store gets eye contact and a "Hey, how are you?"

Phone use during shifts is a privilege, not a right. It is allowed only if it does NOT interfere with:

  • Guest awareness and greetings
  • Eye contact with approaching customers
  • Speed of service
  • Food handling and safety

Phones may NOT be used:

  • While serving or acknowledging guests
  • During rush periods
  • When a guest is nearby or looking at the store
  • With headphones or AirPods - ever

Heads up. Eyes forward. Phones away. Every guest acknowledged.

8. Dress Code Arrive ready for the shift +
  • PWI shirt is required at all times. If you arrive without your shirt, you will be given one at a cost of $20.
  • Sneakers are required for safety and hygiene.
  • No sunglasses while working.
  • No chewing gum while working.
  • Pants or shorts must be clean, presentable, and no shorter than mid-thigh. No clothing or hats with profanity or inappropriate graphics. This is a family-friendly environment.
9. Punctuality & Breaks Be on time and follow break rules +
  • It is your responsibility to know your hours of employment and to be there 5 minutes before your shift starts. This is common courtesy for the worker you are replacing.
  • Repeated tardiness can and will result in dismissal.
  • 4 to 6 hour shift: No break.
  • No breaks between 7 PM and 12 AM. No exceptions.
  • Always ask your shift manager before taking a break.
10. Cash Register Write mistakes down and tell the manager +

If you make a mistake on the register, do not try to fix it yourself. Write it down in the provided notebook and let the manager know.

When a customer pays with a non-cash method (card, Apple Pay, etc.), always ask: "Would you like a receipt?"

11. Inventory & Cups Protect inventory and record damage +
  • Never throw away cups. Wash them.
  • Damaged cups must be kept and marked down in the provided notebook.
  • Extra cups for customers: use blank cups only, never PWI logo cups.
12. Bathroom Restroom access policy for customers +
  • Only employees are allowed behind the counter.
  • If a customer asks to use the restroom, say: "I am sorry but we do not have public restrooms."
13. Personal Belongings Keep personal items out of the work area +

All personal belongings should be kept in the back of the store.

14. Workplace Rules Keep it professional +
  • Always be visible at the front counter, facing the boardwalk. Customers should see a friendly face ready to help - not an empty counter.
  • Do not put your feet on the counter.
  • No smoking in or around the workplace.
  • Please discourage your friends from hanging out around the counter.
  • If standing around, ask yourself: is the floor clean? Counters wiped? Machines presentable? Supplies stocked? If everything is good, relax and wait for the next customer.
15. Employee Drinks Pay right away for staff soda +

Employees may purchase soda at $2.00. Payment must be made immediately.

16. Common Questions Quick answers for frequent customer questions +

"What is PWI?"

PWI (Polish Water Ice) is smoother and creamier than regular water ice.

"Why is it called Polish?"

The founder had Polish roots, so he named it Polish Water Ice.

"What's the difference between water ice and ice cream?"

Water ice is a dairy-free, fruit-flavored frozen treat eaten with a spoon. Ice cream is soft serve.

"Can I mix flavors?"

Yes. Guests can mix any 2 PWI flavors in one cup.

"What's a Polish Freeze?"

It is a combo cup with water ice on one side and ice cream on the other.

"What's a Chiller?"

A Chiller is blended ice cream with candy mix-ins, similar to a DQ Blizzard.

"Is water ice dairy-free?"

Yes. PWI, Slushie, and Ice Cap are all dairy-free.

"What's your most popular flavor?"

Cotton Candy is the #1 selling PWI flavor.
